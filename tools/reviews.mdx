  <Tab title="Hosting">
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Vercel">
        <Badge>9/10</Badge> Vercel excels at static sites and serverless functions with zero-config deployments.

**Pros:** Instant previews, edge functions, tight Next.js integration, free tier

**Cons:** Cold starts on serverless, vendor lock-in for advanced features

<Tip>Best for teams shipping Next.js or static sites who want minimal DevOps.</Tip>
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Railway">
        <Badge>8/10</Badge> Railway offers simple full-stack hosting with databases, cron jobs, and monorepos.

**Pros:** One-click PostgreSQL, GitHub integration, straightforward pricing

**Cons:** Fewer regions than competitors, less mature than Heroku-era options

<Tip>Best for indie hackers and small teams who need a database alongside their app.</Tip>
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="DigitalOcean App Platform">
        <Badge>7/10</Badge> App Platform provides PaaS-style deployment with predictable pricing.

**Pros:** Simple pricing, managed databases, good for Laravel and Node

**Cons:** Slower builds than Vercel, fewer edge features

<Tip>Best for teams who prefer straightforward billing and Laravel/Node backends.</Tip>
      </Accordion>

  <Tab title="Databases">
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="PlanetScale">
        <Badge>9/10</Badge> PlanetScale is a MySQL-compatible serverless database with branching.

**Pros:** Branch-based workflow, auto-scaling, no connection limits

**Cons:** No foreign keys, MySQL dialect only

<Tip>Best for teams who want Git-like database workflows and scalable MySQL.</Tip>
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Neon">
        <Badge>8/10</Badge> Neon is a serverless PostgreSQL with branching and autoscaling.

**Pros:** Full Postgres, branch per PR, generous free tier

**Cons:** Newer than Aurora/RDS, some extensions limited

<Tip>Best for PostgreSQL users who want branching and serverless scaling.</Tip>
      </Accordion>

  <Tab title="CI/CD">
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="GitHub Actions">
        <Badge>9/10</Badge> GitHub Actions integrates CI/CD directly into your repository.

**Pros:** Free minutes, marketplace actions, secrets management

**Cons:** YAML can get verbose, slower startup than some runners

<Tip>Best for teams already on GitHub who want built-in CI without extra services.</Tip>
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="GitLab CI">
        <Badge>8/10</Badge> GitLab CI provides pipelines, security scanning, and deployment features.

**Pros:** All-in-one platform, built-in registry, pipeline templates

**Cons:** Runner management overhead, learning curve for advanced features

<Tip>Best for teams using GitLab who want integrated DevOps in one place.</Tip>
      </Accordion>

  <Tab title="Monitoring">
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Better Stack">
        <Badge>8/10</Badge> Better Stack offers uptime monitoring, logs, and incident management.

**Pros:** Clean UI, status pages, affordable for small teams

**Cons:** Fewer integrations than Datadog, newer product

<Tip>Best for startups who need uptime + logs without enterprise pricing.</Tip>
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Sentry">
        <Badge>9/10</Badge> Sentry focuses on error tracking and performance monitoring.

**Pros:** Excellent stack traces, release health, wide SDK support

**Cons:** Can get expensive at scale, noisy without tuning

<Tip>Best for teams who prioritize catching and fixing errors quickly.</Tip>
